# Riftbound Rules Engine — Domain Glossary

This glossary defines the rulebook vocabulary used throughout the codebase. Module names,
type names, and comments draw from these terms. When in doubt, prefer the term used here
over an informal synonym.

---

## Game Structure

**Match** — A best-of-3 series between two players. The first player to win 2 games wins the Match.

**Game** — A single complete game within a Match, starting from setup and ending when a player reaches the Victory Score (8 points) with strictly more points than the opponent.

**Turn** — One player's complete action sequence: Start Phase → Channel Phase → Main Phase → Ending Phase.

**Phase** — One of the four segments of a turn: `Start`, `Channel`, `Main`, `Ending`.

**Cleanup** — The steps run at the start of the Ending Phase: scoring, HOT drain, win-condition check, per-turn field resets, discard-to-hand-size, temporary effect removal.

---

## Zones

**Battlefield Zone** — One of the contested scoring locations. Each player brings three Battlefield cards; one from each player is selected per game.

**Legend Zone** — Where each player's Legend card resides for the duration of the game.

**Champion Zone** — Where each player's Chosen Champion resides.

**Base** — The zone where a player's own units and gear are stationed (not yet contesting a battlefield).

**Main Deck** — The primary card draw source; 40–60 cards, ordered top → bottom.

**Rune Deck** — The rune card source; exactly 10 cards, ordered top → bottom.

**Rune Pool** — Up to 4 rune slots; filled from the Rune Deck each turn during the Channel Phase.

---

## Card Types

**Legend** — A unique, always-in-play card in the Legend Zone. Defines a player's faction and abilities.

**Chosen Champion** — The player's selected Champion card, residing in the Champion Zone.

**Unit** — A creature card played to a Battlefield or Base.

**Gear** — An equipment card attached to a unit, granting bonuses.

**Spell** — A one-shot effect card; moves to discard after resolution.

**Token** — A created card instance with no corresponding deck entry; banished when it leaves play.

**Rune** — A resource card that fills a Rune Slot when channeled.

---

## Resources

**Rune** — A channeled resource; fills one Rune Slot per card.

**Energy** — A fast resource produced by Rune abilities; spent to activate fast effects.

**Power** — A slow resource produced by tapping Runes; spent to play cards or activate slow abilities.

**XP** — Experience points accumulated on certain cards; spent to unlock ability tiers.

---

## Resolution

**Chain** — The open window in which either player may play cards or activate abilities in response to one another. Governs who holds Priority.

**Showdown** — A structured combat or control contest at a specific Battlefield. Has its own Priority-like system called Focus.

**Priority** — The right to act in the Chain. Tracked as `chain.priority`. Passes when the active player `PassPriority`.

**Focus** — The right to act within a Showdown. Tracked as `chain.focus`. Separate from Priority; never conflated (core rules 327–348).

**Resolution Stack** — An explicit serializable stack of `StackFrame` values that tracks multi-step resolution across `submit()` calls. Replaces a live call stack.

**DecisionRequest** — A pause point where the engine requires a specific player to provide input before it can continue. Set as `state.pendingDecision`.

---

## Resolution Sequences

**FEPR** — The four-step chain resolution loop: **F**inalize → **E**xecute → **P**ass → **R**esolve. One full FEPR cycle resolves the top chain item and checks for the next.

**HOT FEPR** — **H**andle **O**utstanding **T**asks → FEPR. The HOT step drains triggered abilities from the `hotQueue` before each FEPR cycle.

---

## Scoring

**Conquer** — Score a point by changing control of a Battlefield during your turn.

**Hold** — Score a point by controlling a Battlefield at both the start and end of your turn's cleanup.

**Winning Point** — The mechanic (core rule 466.1.b) that limits how many points a player at 7+ points can score via Conquer in a single cleanup: only scores if every Battlefield was scored that turn; otherwise draws a card.

**Victory Score** — 8 points. A player wins when they reach 8 points with strictly more points than their opponent at the end of cleanup.

---

## Engine Concepts

**Effect Program** — A compiled `EffectNode[]` tree representing a card's abilities. Produced by the card compiler at build time; the engine never parses card text at runtime.

**Layers System** — The 5-layer dependency graph used by `RulesQuery` to resolve a card's effective stats. Uses Kahn's topological sort to evaluate in dependency order.

**Player View** — A projection of `GameState` for a specific player, with all hidden information redacted. Generated by `viewFor(state, playerId, catalog)`.

**Seeded RNG** — Mulberry32, a 32-bit integer state RNG. All randomness in the engine flows through it: deck shuffles, turn-order coin flip, random Battlefield selection. The seed advances immutably — callers replace `state.rng` with the returned `next`.
